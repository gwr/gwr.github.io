• The largest U.S. non-profit home health care provider elected to implement a multi-disciplinary-team-based approach for patient care and by so doing, shift the structure and culture of the entire organization dramatically. In collaboration with Trinity Partners Consulting, we trained internal consultants and worked side-by-side with them and senior leaders to design and execute a strategy to accomplish this goal. This resulted in the successful creation of 50 new teams in a single year. Significant improvements occurred in clinical care, patient satisfaction, employee performance and overall financial results. Ongoing leadership development continues to strengthen the organization’s capacity to think and act more effectively.

  • For a major paper company, we worked with a newly formed IT oversight team to generate the vision, strategy, processes, operating principles and values that enabled team members to overcome their functional and territorial allegiances. As a result, the technical architecture, standards and guidelines developed by the team were exceptionally well received throughout the company, and the team was able to gain credibility and approval for strategic IT projects that remained sidetracked in the past.

  • For a Fortune 100 manufacturer, we trained a group of internal consultants to build organizational learning capacity in one 5000 person unit. We also trained a small group of facilitators in systems thinking and helped them strategize how they would work with their internal clients to improve the way people think, act, and collaborate. They are now dealing with a variety of organizational issues and have expanded their work throughout the company.

  • A South African oil company decided to include all the employees in setting the future direction and goals of the company. The senior management felt it was critical to get the input of everyone as well as to give them an opportunity to work with others in the organization they normally had little contact with. This work was accomplished through designing and delivering a series of programs co-led by internal consultants that we trained. The participation of the workforce in these programs led to a high level of enrollment and commitment to the company’s goals. Two other key results of the workshops were a significant increase in the collaboration of employees and a relaxing of racial tension.

  • For a major petroleum company in Latin America, we implemented a new organization design. This work included coaching executives, line mangers and work teams in creating and coordinating overall corporate and facility goals, and in helping those individuals and teams develop the new working behaviors and norms required for success in the new organization structure that united maintenance with operations in team based production units. To date the successful implementation of the structure and shift in norms from volume to productivity has yielded eighty million of dollars in annual savings.
